CHIPPING

Choose the right club: Typically, a golfer will use a wedge or a short iron for a chip shot. The choice of club will depend on the distance to the hole, the type of lie, and the slope of the ground. Golfers should select a club that will allow them to get the ball onto the green and rolling towards the hole with control.

Position the ball: Golfers should position the ball in the center of their stance, with their weight slightly forward and their hands ahead of the ball. This will help to create a descending blow and generate backspin, which will allow the ball to stop more quickly when it lands on the green.

Keep the swing compact: Golfers should use a short, compact swing for a chip shot, with a smooth tempo and a descending blow. The swing should be controlled, with a focus on keeping the hands ahead of the ball at impact.

PUTTING

Choose the right putter: There are many different types of putters available, each with their own characteristics. Golfers should choose a putter that feels comfortable and suits their stroke. Some putters are designed to help with alignment, while others are designed to promote a smooth, consistent stroke.

Set up correctly: A good putting stroke starts with a good setup. Golfers should aim to keep their feet shoulder-width apart, with their weight evenly distributed. They should position the ball in the center of their stance, with their eyes directly over the ball.

Read the green: Before hitting the putt, golfers should take time to read the slope and speed of the green. They should look for any hills or valleys that could affect the ball's trajectory, and try to visualize the path of the ball towards the hole. By reading the green correctly, golfers can choose the right line and give themselves the best chance of sinking the putt.
